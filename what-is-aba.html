What is ABA Therapy?

Applied Behavior Analysis is the most researched, evidence-based approach available for autism and developmental differences. Here's what you need to know.

The Science

Applied Behavior Analysis

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is a scientific approach to understanding behavior — how it is learned, what maintains it, and how it can be changed. It applies principles derived from decades of research to help individuals develop meaningful skills and reduce behaviors that interfere with learning and quality of life.

ABA is endorsed by the American Psychological Association, the U.S. Surgeon General, and the American Academy of Pediatrics as an effective, evidence-based treatment for autism spectrum disorder.

At Avanza ABA, we implement ABA in its most ethical, compassionate, and individualized form — focused on what each person cares about, what motivates them, and where they want to grow.

🧩

"The science of behavior, applied with heart."

What ABA Addresses

Skills ABA helps build

ABA is not one-size-fits-all. Every plan targets the areas that matter most to your family — from communication to daily independence.

💬

Communication

Building verbal and non-verbal communication — from first words and functional requests to conversational language and social communication.

🤝

Social Skills

Learning to play, share, make friends, read social cues, and navigate peer interactions with confidence.

🏡

Daily Living Skills

Dressing, hygiene, cooking, money management — the practical skills that build independence across every age.

😌

Emotional Regulation

Understanding and managing emotions, coping with transitions, and building flexibility in thinking and behavior.

📚

Academic Readiness

Foundational skills like attention, following instructions, and learning readiness that support classroom success.

⚡

Reducing Challenging Behaviors

Understanding the function of challenging behaviors and replacing them with safer, more effective alternatives.
